What CanDash is

CanDash is an OBD-II diagnostics and gauge-display app for car enthusiasts. It connects to a third-party OBD-II adapter plugged into your vehicle’s diagnostic port and reads data using the ELM327 protocol. It is intended for car owners who want to view live vehicle data, customise their dashboard, and read or clear diagnostic trouble codes on a vehicle they own or are authorised to access.

Vehicle responsibility

CanDash reads data from your vehicle’s ECU and, in the Diagnostics tab, can clear stored fault codes. Clearing fault codes erases information that may be important for diagnosing a fault. Use this feature only if you understand what you are doing. We are not responsible for any consequence of clearing fault codes, including but not limited to:

  • Loss of diagnostic information needed by a mechanic.
  • Loss of emissions readiness status, which may cause your vehicle to fail an inspection, MOT, or smog test until drive cycles complete.
  • Any vehicle behaviour that follows a code clear.

The data shown by CanDash depends on what your vehicle’s ECU reports and on the accuracy of the OBD-II adapter you use. Readings may be inaccurate, delayed, or missing entirely. Do not rely on CanDash for safety-critical decisions. Always cross-reference with your vehicle’s own instruments and a qualified mechanic where appropriate.

Manufacturer-specific (extended) PIDs and vehicle packs

CanDash includes vehicle-specific PID packs (for example, Jaguar Land Rover, Ford, VAG, Mazda, GM) that read manufacturer-specific data using extended OBD-II requests. These PIDs are derived from publicly available information and community research, not from manufacturer technical documentation we have a licence to. They have been tested where possible but we cannot guarantee they will work, or work correctly, on every vehicle, model year, or trim level. The performance of any specific PID on your specific vehicle is not guaranteed.

Diagnostic depth and database coverage

CanDash performs universal OBD-II diagnostics (engine fault codes, MIL status, emissions readiness, live engine data) on any vehicle built 1996 or later that complies with the OBD-II standard. Deeper diagnostic features — including module discovery, per-module fault code reading, and module identification — are only available on vehicles for which we have published a diagnostic database entry. The list of supported vehicles grows over time as we add new database entries; updates are delivered automatically without requiring an app update.

If your vehicle is not yet in the database, you will still be able to use the standard universal OBD-II features, but the deeper module-level scan will not be offered for that vehicle.
